# Hospital-emergency-room-
Title
Hospital-emergency-room
Project Introduction
Objective: Analyze Emergency Room operations to identify efficiency gaps and improve patient experience
Scope: Complete month of September 2023 (30-day dataset)
Data Size: 469 unique patient records with comprehensive metrics
Focus Areas: Patient volume, wait times, admission rates, and satisfaction scores
Analysis Steps
Descriptive Analysis: Summary statistics for patient volumes, wait times, and satisfaction
Trend Analysis: Daily patterns to identify peak hours and volume fluctuations
Demographic Segmentation: Analysis across age groups, gender, and department referrals
Correlation Study: Relationship between patient volume, wait times, and satisfaction scores
Key Insights & Findings
Admission Rate: 53.1% admitted, 46.9% discharged (slight majority for hospitalization)
Attendance Punctuality: 54.8% arrive delayed (257 of 469), only 45.2% arrive on-time
Patient Volumes Peak: September 15th and 22nd recorded highest attendance (26 and 23 patients)
Wait Time Variation: Range 28.38 to 43.47 minutes; inconsistent service delivery across days
Low Satisfaction Scores: Average 4.98/10 indicates significant patient dissatisfaction (below 50%)
Gender Distribution: Nearly equal split (Male: 53.3%, Female: 46.7%)
Age Distribution: Fairly uniform (52-66 patients per age bracket); highest volume in 40-49 age group (66 patients)
Department Load: 260 patients (55.4%) require no specialist referral; 107 (22.8%) to General Practice
Specialist Demand: Orthopedics (48), Physiotherapy (17), Cardiology (13) are top referral departments
